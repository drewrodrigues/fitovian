Why Diets are not the Solution?

Studies currently show that this year alone within the United States over 100 million people will diet. That is equivalent to the population of California, Texas, Florida, Arizona, and Colorado combined. These dieters will average 4 to 5 attempts to lose weight within this year. Unfortunately, an overwhelming majority of these dieters will not achieve their desired goals. And for the ones that do succeed at losing weight studies show that approximately 85% of them will end up gaining the weight back and even more. That statistic alone speaks for itself. It is very clear that diets may work in the short-term but long-term they are not the solution.

Diets are usually geared toward helping people drop weight quickly within a short period of time but offer little to no long term solution when it comes to maintaining a healthy weight. Diets also fall short due to the fact that people are unique from one another and many diets do not take that into account. This is not to say that diets cannot be an effective short-term solution because we do believe that some may work. However, to maintain long-term results there is much more needed than simply a temporary diet modification.

We understand that many people diet for a multitude of reasons such as health, physical appearance, increased energy, etc. However, the reality is that diets simply are not an effective solution to maintaining long term results. If temporarily weight loss is what people seek, then there are thousands of diets available. The best way to reach your weight loss goal and maintain a healthy weight is to be educated, motivated, disciplined, and most importantly form healthy habits.

Our program is designed to help you focus on creating long term healthy habits that promote weight loss and help you maintain a healthy weight.
